The Philosophy

Foundations First

The AI/ML landscape is dazzling — and I'm fully committed to it. But I've made a deliberate choice to spend my sophomore year doing something unfashionable: going slow.

"You can't debug what you don't understand. You can't optimise what you haven't first grasped by hand."

Before I train a model, I want to understand the algebra of computation — how memory works, how algorithms breathe, why a tree is faster than a list for certain problems. At Parul University, I'm pairing my AI/ML coursework with deep dives into Data Structures, Computational Logic, and Core Programming paradigms.

The certificates, the hackathon, the projects — they're not the goal. They're mile markers on a longer road toward building systems that are not just powerful, but genuinely understood.

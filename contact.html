About Me

I'm Hetarth Salat, a second-year B.Tech student majoring in Computer Science Engineering with a specialisation in Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning at Parul University, Vadodara.

I grew up in Gujarat with a persistent curiosity about how things work — not just at the surface, but all the way down. That curiosity pulled me toward computers, and then toward computer science: not just using the tools, but understanding the principles that make them possible.

"I'm not in a rush to specialise. I'd rather spend time now building a foundation so solid that every advanced topic I encounter later clicks into place."

My day-to-day at Parul is a balance of formal coursework and deliberate self-study. In class: AI/ML theory, mathematics, and software engineering principles. On my own: coding data structures in C from scratch, building OOP intuition in Java, and using Python for anything that needs to move fast. The Vadodara Hackathon 6.0 gave me my first taste of engineering in a real-time, high-pressure team environment — and I want more of that.

I'm drawn to the long game. My goal isn't to ship fast — it's to build well, understand deeply, and eventually contribute to AI systems that are interpretable, robust, and grounded in real computer science.

Outside of code, I enjoy reading about the history of computing, understanding how major paradigms (OOP, functional, concurrent) evolved, and occasionally breaking something just to learn how to fix it.

Currently open to: internship opportunities, project collaborations, open-source contributions, and mentorship from experienced engineers and researchers.
